Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before booking a guided trip.

Where do you guide?

We guide at Ash Branch (Powell County), RRGCC-managed crags, Muir Valley, and other permitted venues throughout the Red River Gorge. The one exception is the North Gorge, which falls under National Forest land and requires a different permit structure. The specific crag is chosen based on conditions, group level, and availability.

Do I need prior experience?

Not for the Intro to Outdoor Climbing program. It's designed for complete beginners with zero outdoor experience. The Sport Lead Clinic requires comfort on top-rope. The Trad & Anchor Building course assumes you're already comfortable leading sport routes. Each program page has full prerequisite details.

What gear is provided?

All climbing gear is included. You don't need to bring or rent anything to climb:

  • Climbing shoes
  • Harness
  • Helmet
  • Ropes and all anchor equipment
  • Belay device (GriGri or ATC)
  • Quickdraws and PAS
  • Full trad rack (cams and nuts) for the Trad & Anchor course

Prefer to use your own shoes, harness, or helmet? Bring them along.

What do I need to bring?

  • Minimum 2 liters of water
  • Weather-appropriate clothing (layers)
  • Crag lunch and snacks
  • Sleeping system rated to 40°F (if camping overnight)

Just show up ready to climb. We handle the rest.

Where do we meet?

The default meetup is Miguel's Pizza campground in Slade, KY, the unofficial base camp of the Red River Gorge. Camping is available on-site with breakfast and dinner at Miguel's. The exact crag meetup will be confirmed before your trip day.

What is your cancellation policy?

We offer full refunds for cancellations made 14+ days before your trip. Cancellations within 14 days receive a 50% refund or full credit toward a future booking.

Are you certified and insured?

Yes. Your guide holds an AMGA Single Pitch Instructor (SPI) certification and a Wilderness First Responder (WFR) certification including CPR/AED. Crag College Guides LLC is fully insured for professional guiding operations at the Red River Gorge.
