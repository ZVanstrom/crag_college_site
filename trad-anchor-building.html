Build the Foundation for Trad Climbing

The Trad & Anchor Building course is a full-day deep dive into gear-protected climbing. You'll work through cam and nut placement in real cracks, learn to evaluate natural features for anchors, and practice building redundant equalized systems from scratch, all under direct guidance from an AMGA SPI at permitted Red River Gorge crags.

🧗 What You'll Learn

  • Cam and nut selection and placement in real cracks
  • Evaluating placements for direction of pull
  • Identifying natural features (horns, threads, trees)
  • Building redundant, equalized anchor systems
  • Leave No Trace practices for trad climbing

🎒 What to Bring

  • Minimum 2 liters of water
  • Weather-appropriate layers
  • Crag lunch and snacks
  • Sleeping system rated to 40°F if camping
  • Your own shoes, harness, or helmet if you prefer to use personal gear

✅ What's Provided

  • All climbing gear included: ropes, harness, helmet, belay device, full trad rack (cams and nuts), and anchor material
  • AMGA SPI-certified instruction throughout

📍 Where We Climb

  • RRGCC land (Red River Gorge Climbers' Coalition)
  • Ash Branch (Powell County)
  • Crag chosen based on conditions & group level

👤 Prerequisites

  • Comfortable leading sport routes outdoors
  • Familiar with belay devices and anchor systems
  • Prior lead climbing experience required

⏱️ Duration & Group Size

  • Full day: 8–9 hours
  • Max 6 participants per guide
  • Private sessions strongly recommended for this course

📍 Meetup: Miguel's Pizza campground, Slade, KY 40376. We'll drive to the selected crag from there.

🏕️ Camping: On-site camping available at Miguel's. This course pairs well with an overnight trip to maximize crag time.

🎯 Best For: Sport climbers ready to transition to trad, outdoor enthusiasts building toward multi-pitch goals, and climbers wanting a systematic anchor and gear-placement foundation
