Learn to Climb on Real Rock

The Intro to Outdoor Climbing program is your first day outside on real stone. We leave the gym behind and head to permitted crags in the Red River Gorge, where you'll learn how to move on rock, tie into a rope, and belay your partner, all under the direct supervision of an AMGA Single Pitch Instructor.

🧗 What You'll Learn

  • Rock movement fundamentals (footwork, balance, body positioning)
  • Tying a figure-eight follow-through
  • Top-rope belay with a GriGri or ATC
  • Verbal commands and partner communication
  • Route reading and beta

🎒 What to Bring

  • Minimum 2 liters of water
  • Weather-appropriate layers
  • Crag lunch and snacks
  • Sleeping system rated to 40°F if camping
  • Your own shoes, harness, or helmet if you prefer to use personal gear

✅ What's Provided

  • All climbing gear included: ropes, harness, helmet, GriGri/ATC, and anchor equipment
  • AMGA-certified instruction throughout

📍 Where We Climb

  • Ash Branch (Powell County)
  • RRGCC land (Red River Gorge Climbers' Coalition)
  • Muir Valley (private preserve)
  • Crag chosen based on conditions & level

👤 Prerequisites

  • None. This is your first day outside.
  • No gym climbing required
  • All ages welcome (youth with guardian)
  • Reasonable physical fitness recommended

⏱️ Duration & Group Size

  • Half day: ~4 hours
  • Full day: 7–8 hours
  • Max 4 participants per guide
  • Private and semi-private sessions available

📍 Meetup: Miguel's Pizza campground, Slade, KY 40376, the unofficial base camp of the Red River Gorge

🏕️ Camping: On-site camping available at Miguel's. Breakfast and dinner can be purchased there. Bring a sleep system rated to 40°F/4°C.

🎯 Best For: Complete beginners, gym climbers going outside for the first time, families, and groups looking for a memorable first experience on rock
